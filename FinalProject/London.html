Concept

The London System is a popular 1.d4 opening for White which has the reputation of being very solid. The London is considered a system because White can play the same basic setup for almost all of Black's responses. For this reason, the theory on the London is not as extensive as it is for other openings. Like other “System Openings,” white avoids early pawn tension and opts for an easy-to-play set-up that can be used against a wide variety of black responses. White simply develops the bishop actively before playing e3 and c3, and then white can develop his light-squared bishop and get castled.
